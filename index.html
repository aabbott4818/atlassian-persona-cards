Persona 1 · Platform Owner

CIO / Head of Digital

Platform consolidation · Vendor risk · Total cost of tooling
vs. ServiceNow · Microsoft Loop + Copilot + ADO · "We'll standardise on our own platform"

Person · World

Owns the enterprise architecture decision. Sits on a SaaS portfolio of 200+ tools they cannot rationalise. Their last AI initiative — Copilot, an LLM POC, a vendor-led pilot — has not produced a defensible number for the board.

Surface Problem

"We have tool sprawl. Every team has bought their own AI assistant. Nothing is connected, nothing is measurable, and renewals are about to land all at once."

Hidden Flaw

Every AI agent they have bought is reading the wrong substrate. The work — decisions, context, execution state — lives in Jira, Confluence, Slack, SharePoint, Notion, and email. The agents read documents, not the graph of work. So the answers are confident, plausible, and quietly wrong.

Commercial Consequence

The AI line item on the IT budget cannot be defended to the CFO. Tool consolidation has been a board commitment for two budget cycles and the count is still going up. The next CIO conversation is about justifying their job, not their roadmap.

Discovery Questions

  • Of the AI investments you've made in the last 18 months, which one has produced a number you'd put on a board slide?
  • When your AI agents answer a question about work in flight, where are they actually getting that context from?
  • How many systems would have to be connected for an agent to give your COO an honest answer about delivery this week?
  • What's the renewal you're most worried about losing — and what would replacing it require?
  • If you could collapse three categories of tooling into one platform decision in 2026, which three are at the top of your list?

Persona 2 · Throughput Owner

VP / Head of Platform Engineering

Developer productivity · IDP · Build & deploy velocity
vs. GitLab · Linear · Backstage + homegrown · "We'll build our own IDP"

Person · World

Responsible for whether 500-2,000 engineers ship faster this quarter than last. Tracks lead time, cycle time, deployment frequency, PR cycle time. Their CEO has asked them three times this year whether AI is making engineering faster.

Surface Problem

"We've rolled out Copilot. Cycle time hasn't moved. The IDP project has been on the roadmap for two years and isn't shipping. Engineering throughput is still a black box to the business."

Hidden Flaw

Code generation moved fast. The rest of the engineering workflow — design, ticketing, review, deploy, incident — is still manual handoffs across disconnected tools. AI is accelerating the 30% of the work that was already fast and leaving the other 70% untouched.

Commercial Consequence

Engineering capacity is the single biggest line in the company's cost base, and the CEO has stopped believing the productivity number their VP Engineering reports. The next conversation about headcount is going to be uncomfortable.

Discovery Questions

  • What did your cycle time look like 12 months ago versus today? What changed?
  • When an engineer starts a new piece of work, how long from ticket to first commit — and where does most of that time go?
  • How much of the AI you've rolled out actually sits inside the developer's workflow, versus alongside it?
  • Is your IDP a build, a buy, or still a slide?
  • If a PR review took 45% less time, where would that capacity show up — more features, fewer engineers, or higher quality?

Triggers · When they're in-market now

Stalled IDP project Engineering efficiency mandate Copilot ROI review New VP Eng < 12 months Post-IPO efficiency push

Buying Signals

  • They talk about cycle time without prompting
  • They reference DORA, SPACE, or DevEx by name
  • They ask about Compass or Rovo Code Reviewer specifically
  • They volunteer their internal IDP frustration

Where Atlassian Applies

Compass as the IDP layer. Jira + Confluence as the work substrate Rovo can actually read. Rovo Code Reviewer, Code Planner, and DevEx agents as throughput multipliers — measured in lead-time-to-start, not feature checkboxes.

First 90 Seconds

"The engineering leaders I'm speaking to all rolled out Copilot last year and almost none of them can point to a cycle-time number that moved. The pattern I keep seeing is that AI accelerated the coding and left the workflow untouched. Is that the conversation you're having internally, or is your bottleneck somewhere else?"

Deploy This When · Proof Point

Atlassian's own engineering team saw a 45% improvement in PR cycle time using Rovo Code Reviewer. Across Jira customers, developers who adopted Rovo AI started work 30% faster, saw a 35% reduction in Lead Time to Start, and recovered +1 productive day per month per developer. Domino's used Compass + Jira + Rovo to consolidate their internal developer platform. Atlassian engineering blog · Domino's Compass case

Bridge Line · To the Next Stakeholder

"The number that lands hardest in the boardroom isn't cycle time — it's the COO's confidence in the delivery dashboard. Would it help if the next conversation included whoever owns that?"

Persona 3 · Service Owner

Head of ITSM / Service Operations

Ticket deflection · MTTR · Agent capacity · ServiceNow decision
vs. ServiceNow · Freshservice · BMC Helix · Status quo (sunk-cost ServiceNow renewal)

Person · World

Runs the service desk for 5,000-50,000 internal users. Spends 50-70% of their IT budget on ServiceNow. Has been told to deflect more tickets, raise CSAT, and reduce headcount — all at the same time.

Surface Problem

"ServiceNow is too expensive but we can't rip it out. Every AI deflection vendor wants a fresh integration. Tier-1 ticket volume keeps growing and we cannot hire our way out of it."

Hidden Flaw

The ServiceNow renewal is being defended on switching cost, not on value. Meanwhile, every Rovo customer at JSM Premium gets Virtual Service Agent with 1,000 conversations/month included — at a fraction of the per-agent cost. The "we can't migrate" position is getting more expensive every quarter it's repeated.

Commercial Consequence

A 500-agent ITSM platform on ServiceNow runs $200-400 per agent per month fully loaded. The same workload on JSM Premium at $51.42 with Rovo bundled is a 60-80% TCO reduction in year one. The CFO will eventually do this math. The question is whether ITSM leadership runs the migration or has it run for them.

Discovery Questions

  • When does your ServiceNow renewal land, and what was the uplift last time?
  • What percentage of tier-1 tickets could be deflected if the agent could see across Jira, Confluence, and your knowledge base in one place?
  • How much of your ServiceNow estate is genuinely differentiated workflow versus standard ITSM you're paying premium pricing for?
  • If you had to move 500 agents to a new platform, what would be the actual blocker — data, change management, or the renewal cycle?
  • Who owns the ServiceNow vs. alternative decision in your organisation — and have they done the TCO math recently?

Triggers · When they're in-market now

ServiceNow renewal within 12 months Recent ServiceNow uplift ITSM cost-reduction mandate Existing Jira footprint Tier-1 volume growth pressure

Buying Signals

  • They name the ServiceNow renewal date unprompted
  • They ask about Virtual Service Agent pricing
  • They mention dev and IT being on different platforms today
  • They volunteer their deflection target

Where Atlassian Applies

Jira Service Management Enterprise as the ServiceNow alternative. Virtual Service Agent for tier-1 deflection. Assets for CMDB. Rovo for agent-side knowledge retrieval. Service Collection — which crossed $1B ARR and is growing 30%+ YoY — as the commercial vehicle.

First 90 Seconds

"Most ITSM leaders I'm speaking to are in the same trap: ServiceNow is too expensive to defend and too embedded to rip out. The pattern that's starting to break that is teams running their Jira-native workflows on JSM and only keeping ServiceNow for the genuinely differentiated 20%. Is that economics conversation alive in your org, or is the renewal still on autopilot?"

Deploy This When · Proof Point

Saint-Gobain cut ITSM cost by 70% in year one migrating from ServiceNow to JSM, with a projected 82% reduction by year three. 1E (now part of TeamViewer) reduced ticket handling time by up to 80% using Rovo agents. Persona 4 · Delivery Owner

Chief Transformation Officer / COO

Cross-functional execution · OKR delivery · "Why does the board not know the truth"
vs. Monday · Asana · ClickUp · PowerPoint + a project management office

Person · World

Owns the question "why are projects slipping?" at the board level. Has 30-60 strategic initiatives in flight. Tuesday-morning project review pulls status from six tools and a deck somebody rebuilds every week.

Surface Problem

"I cannot get a single trustworthy view of delivery. Every team reports green until the project is two months late. Our transformation programme is supposed to be data-driven and it's running on PowerPoint."

Hidden Flaw

The riskiest initiatives are the ones with the most cross-functional dependencies — and those are exactly the ones invisible to any single team's tool. Status reporting is bottoms-up and weeks behind reality. By the time delivery risk surfaces, the board has already been briefed twice that things are on track.

Commercial Consequence

Board-visible OKR delivery slips quarterly. The COO's credibility erodes one missed milestone at a time. The next CEO conversation about "what's actually shipping" is one they cannot prepare for, because nobody owns the connected view.

Discovery Questions

  • When the board asks "are we on track" on Tuesday, what's the actual source of the answer — and how stale is it?
  • Of the projects that slipped last year, how many were visible as at-risk more than four weeks before the slip?
  • Where does context about a cross-functional initiative actually live — Slack, Jira, Confluence, email, or somebody's head?
  • If an AI agent could answer "what's the real status of project X" in 30 seconds, what would change about your operating cadence?
  • Who owns the single trustworthy delivery view today — and is that person being asked to do it on top of their day job?

Triggers · When they're in-market now

Recent board surprise on delivery New COO < 12 months Transformation programme launch PMO restructure "Operating cadence" overhaul

Buying Signals

  • They use the phrase "system of record" or "single source of truth" without prompting
  • They name a recent project that slipped without warning
  • They ask whether Rovo can answer cross-functional status questions
  • They volunteer their Tuesday operating review pain

Where Atlassian Applies

Jira + Confluence as the system of work. Teamwork Graph as the connected delivery state. Rovo as the COO-side agent that answers "what's actually happening" in 30 seconds rather than 30 emails. Atlas (Atlassian's strategy-to-execution layer) as the board-visible reporting surface.

First 90 Seconds

"Most COOs I'm speaking to share one quiet frustration: the riskiest initiatives are the ones with the most dependencies, and those are exactly the ones nobody can see in real time. The pattern that breaks it is having an agent that can read across the entire work substrate, not just one team's tool. Is that the conversation you're having in your operating reviews, or is your problem somewhere else?"

Deploy This When · Proof Point

Lufthansa Systems collapsed multiple project-level service desks into one horizontal JSM Customer Service Portal that now serves 300+ airlines through a single platform with native knowledge-base self-service. ServiceRocket used Rovo to build a 20-year account intelligence dashboard in three minutes by querying 61 sources across the Teamwork Graph. Lufthansa Systems case · ServiceRocket Team '26 recap

Persona 5 · Capital Owner

CFO / Finance Business Partner to Tech

Software portfolio cost · AI ROI scrutiny · Contract consolidation
vs. "Do nothing" · Excel and a procurement freeze · "Wait for the AI hype to settle"

Person · World

Looking at a software portfolio that grew 40-60% over three years, a flat-to-down headcount plan, and an AI line item with no defensible ROI. Has been asked by the CEO and the board for a credible AI productivity number — twice.

Surface Problem

"Software spend is up. AI spend is the fastest-growing line in IT. I cannot tell the board what we got for either of them. Renewals are coming and I need a portfolio strategy, not another vendor."

Hidden Flaw

The AI line on the IT budget is being treated as a new category, but most of it is actually replicating capability the company already paid for in its existing platforms. Atlassian customers, for example, already have Rovo bundled into Premium and Enterprise editions — the AI ROI is sitting in seats they're already paying for, going un-activated.

Commercial Consequence

Three to five point tools could be eliminated by standardising on Atlassian as the system of work. The Data Center to Cloud migration is going to force a budget conversation in 2026 anyway. The choice is whether Finance leads that conversation with a portfolio strategy or has it imposed on them by IT.

Discovery Questions

  • What's your current annual spend across Atlassian, ServiceNow, Notion, Monday/Asana, and standalone AI tools combined?
  • Of the AI spend in the last 18 months, what's the ROI number you'd put in front of the audit committee?
  • What's the Data Center to Cloud decision looking like for you, and what's the deadline forcing it?
  • If you consolidated three categories of tooling into one platform, what would the year-one savings need to be to make the migration credible?
  • Who else in the organisation is most exposed if the AI ROI question goes unanswered — and would they be useful in this conversation?

Triggers · When they're in-market now

Budget cycle < 90 days out Software audit / portfolio review Data Center migration decision New CFO < 12 months AI ROI board ask

Buying Signals

  • They ask about consolidation math, not features
  • They name three tools they want to kill
  • They volunteer their Data Center migration timeline
  • They want a TCO model on paper before the next conversation

Where Atlassian Applies

Atlassian Cloud Enterprise as the consolidation target. Teamwork Collection as the AI monetisation vehicle bundled into existing seats (not a separate line item). Service Collection as the ServiceNow displacement. The Data Center to Cloud migration timeline as the forcing function for a portfolio decision.

First 90 Seconds

"Most CFOs I'm speaking to share a specific frustration: AI spend is the fastest-growing line in IT and the only one without a defensible ROI number. The pattern that's emerging is that the AI ROI was sitting in platforms they were already paying for — and they were buying it again as a separate line. Is that conversation happening in your portfolio review, or is your priority somewhere else this quarter?"

Tension 1

Tool sprawl & the consolidation overdue

CIO CFO

What both personas are feeling

Software spend grew faster than headcount, AI added another layer of point tools, and renewals are now landing within months of each other. Neither persona has a portfolio strategy that survives the next board meeting.

"We've been promising to rationalise the stack for two years and the tool count is still going up."

Where Atlassian lands

Atlassian Cloud Enterprise + Teamwork Collection as a consolidation target that absorbs the AI line item rather than adding to it. The Data Center to Cloud forcing function makes 2026 the budget cycle this gets decided in.

Tension 2

AI bought, ROI missing

CIO COO CFO

What all three personas are feeling

Copilot, an LLM POC, a vendor pilot — none have produced a board-defensible number. The board is asking, the auditors are asking, and nobody owns the answer.

"We've spent on AI. I cannot tell you what we got."

Rovo isn't an AI feature being sold on top of the platform — it's the value of having built the substrate. The 2x ARR growth differential reported in Atlassian's Q3 FY26 letter is the proof point that AI ROI requires connected work, not better models.

Code generation moved fast. Cycle time didn't. The CEO has stopped believing the productivity reports and the board has stopped asking nicely.

Rovo Code Reviewer + Compass + Jira AI deliver measurable lead-time reductions (Atlassian internal: 45% PR cycle improvement; Jira customers: 35% Lead Time to Start reduction). Throughput becomes a number, not a vibe.

The ServiceNow renewal is being defended on switching cost, not value. Every uplift makes the math worse. The next conversation is uncomfortable for whoever runs ITSM.

Data Center is forcing a decision they aren't ready for

CIO Platform Eng CFO

The discovery questions, translated to MEDDPICC

Every conversational question on the persona cards maps to a MEDDPICC field. This panel exists for one reason: to show that the soft language above is sitting on top of disciplined qualification underneath.

M · Metrics

The number the buyer puts on a board slide.

Listen for: cycle time, MTTR, deflection %, ARR/seat, TCO delta, defensible AI ROI. From the cards: CIO Q1, Platform Eng Q1, ITSM Q2, COO Q2, CFO Q2.

E · Economic Buyer

The signer of a £500k-£3M+ ACV decision.

Listen for: who owns the renewal, who owns the AI line, who briefs the board. From the cards: CIO Q4, ITSM Q5, CFO Q5.

D · Decision Criteria

What "good" looks like to them, in their language.

Listen for: consolidation math, throughput numbers, TCO model, audit-committee narrative. From the cards: every "Buying Signals" section.

D · Decision Process

How a decision actually happens here.

Listen for: renewal date, budget cycle, board cadence, procurement gate. From the cards: all "Triggers" rows + ITSM Q1, CFO Q1+Q3.

P · Paper Process

The procurement, legal, and security path.

Listen for: security review timeline, paper deadlines, partner-channel preference. Card hook: ask after the bridge to the next stakeholder.

I · Identify Pain

The Hidden Flaw, surfaced in their own words.

Listen for: the pain they volunteer unprompted. From the cards: every "Hidden Flaw" + "First 90 Seconds" pairing.

C · Champion

Someone with power and motive to sell internally.

Listen for: "I've been trying to get this on the agenda" / "Finance and I have been talking about this". Card hook: every "Bridge Line" — it's how you test for a champion in 30 seconds.

C · Competition

The realistic alternative — including "do nothing".

Listen for: ServiceNow renewal language, Copilot rollout, "we'll build our own". From the cards: every red "vs." strip at the top of a persona card.
